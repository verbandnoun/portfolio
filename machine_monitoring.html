In working with monitoring specialists and dealership sales management, I set about the strategy of orienting the discovery process around what I call “success & situations.” This approach allowed me to discover how our users measured their success, how the org measured their success and what situations they encountered in their role-allowing us to focus on key requirements.

This global agriculture manufacturer was focusing on their aftermarket strategy at the time of this project. A key part of that strategy was improving the effectiveness of their dealerships in planning and acting on machine repairs.

And in that they wanted to create a new, fuller product out of one that already existed as a tab within a separate system.

  • What is your role in the dealership and some of the key duties you have? Take me through your day.
  • What are common situations that you encounter day to day?
  • When was the last time you encountered a situation that challenged you?
  • Take me through the steps of how you solve for those?

Scenarios Turned into Nouns, Actions & Themes

When the MMS comes into the office in the morning, they get settled and log in to the system and look for the machines they want to work for the day.

I check what machines I was working the day before to see where I left off. Then, I’ll grab a machine from the list and start to look at the critical alerts and diagnostic codes the machine is sending. I choose machines by the serial numbers. I like to work certain machines so, I’ll grab them based on serial.

The MMS has a sense of what is going on with the machine but, isn’t sure about the limitations on the service agreement, current warranty information or product improvement alerts. They need to find out these key pieces of information and make sure they get to the service manager at the correct location.

Once I send off the information to the service manager, I’ll want to look at that customers fleet and see if there’s other machines with problems that the service department could plan into the work I’ve just sent them. If there is any alerts the customer may have, I’ll want to ge those to that same service manager.

The MMS is done with the prediagnostic phase of a single machine. They’ve passed along the key information the Service Department needs. Now, they’re wondering if the customer has other machines with alerts they need to look at.
